Ally

Ally is an expert app for job seekers searching for quick industry specific insights and actionable advice when applying for the job of their dreams.

This was a project that I developed during my User Experience Design course at Career Foundry. The work started in January 2020. The deliverables were ready for handoff in May same year.

My approach

During the course of the project I was following user-centered design framework that allowed me to organize and plan my tasks without losing focus on the problem and the user.

Research

An important step in the process was to get familiarized with competitors on the market and understand their approach to online advisory. Iâ€™ve looked at two applications: Quikconsult and JustAnswer.

Next, it was time to interview potential users and gather their needs and information about their behaviors. I recruited people who fitted the user profile, that is were actively looking for job opportunities or looked for jobs in the past three months.

Top actionable insights from the interviews:

  • people were most interested in consulting with industry practitioners or recruiters, rather than career coaches
  • people need both written way of communication such as messages or chat and verbal communication phone calls or video conferences
  • people want quick and easy access to consultation, so well functioning and intuitive search functionality is crucial
  • people most value years of experience, expertise and price rate as choice factors

Sitemap

Next, I started thinking about the IA of the and drew a draft sitemap. Afterwards, my initial thoughts were then taken to the test with open card sorting exercise. I asked participants to arrange categories from the draft sitemap into groups. I used the patterns that emerged from the exercise to alter the sitemap where it appeared useful or in some places even necessary. Below is the revised version of the sitemap.

Low- to mid-fidelity wireframes

After grinding through ideas it was time to put pen to paper and start working on sketches and low-fidelity prototype to collect rough drafts of content layouts.

Conceptual low-fidelity wireframes served as a basis for creating mid-fidelity wireframes with Adobe XD. Later, using the wireframes, I drew out the interactions and supplemented them with annotations that served as a static prototype.

After modifying some details and increasing the fidelity of my wireframes, I started setting up interactions for a clickable prototype to use it for testing. At the end of this phase, there were three features ready for testing: send a message to an expert, set up a payment method and book a call with an expert. Review the prototype here
